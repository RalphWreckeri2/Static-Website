Technology in Society

What is Technology?

It encompasses data, the internet, and engineering breakthroughs. In many ways, it has changed civilization. It is the use of scientific knowledge for the betterment of humankind or, to put it another way, for the modification and control of the human environment.

What is Social Media?

It is a type of digital communication that establishes virtual communities for the exchange of knowledge, concepts, private messages, and other materials.

Social Media Tools

These are employed to acquire audiences and acquire data in order to increase sharing and/or content areas. Social media tools can be a feature or an application.

Examples of Social Media Tools

Free expression on Youtube

Imagery on Instagram

Marking yourself on Facebook

Hashtags on Twitter


Bot Accounts

These are automated programs retweeting news stories and quotes, have been used to create discord around controversial topics in many countries.

Crisis Mapping Tool

This generates relevant information for different incidents or real-time situations.

Ushahidi

It is a crisis-mapping tool that collects information from e-mails, text messages, blog posts, and tweets and then maps them, instantly making the information publicly available. (disputed election in Kenya, Africa)

Technology Connects Us with Others

What has Technology did to our society?

Our communication, consumption, and thought processes have all been transformed by technology. Technology has completely changed how we communicate, conduct business, and consume.

Collaborating for the Benefit of Others

  • Web 1.0
      • Static (viewing only)

  • Web 2.0
      • Allows users to easily contribute content and connect with each other. (dynamic/interactive)

  • Web 3.0

    The Importance of Computer Literacy

    What is Computer Literacy?

    Being computer literate means that you are aware of the advantages and disadvantages of computers as well as how to utilize them effectively and securely.

    Being computer savvy can make you a more knowledgeable customer when it comes to purchasing technology services, peripherals, and PCs. You will be in a better position to decide which computers and gadgets you need if you are familiar with computer terminology and stay up to date with technology.

    Computer Security

    It is the defense of computer systems and networks against malicious actor assaults that might lead to theft, destruction, or exposure of confidential information; interruption of or misdirection of the services these systems and networks deliver; or both.

    Avoiding Hackers and Viruses

    Being aware of how they operate and knowing the damage they can cause can help you avoid falling prey to them.

    Protecting your privacy

    Know how to protect yourself from identity theft.

    Understanding the real risks

    being able to separate real privacy and security risks from things you don’t have to worry about.

    Using the web wisely

    Know how to find reliable and accurate information efficiently.

    Avoiding online annoyances

    Avoid spam (unsolicited electronic junk mail).

    Being able to maintain, upgrade, and troubleshoot your computer

    Learning how to care for and maintain your computer and knowing how to diagnose and fix certain problems can save you time and money.

    Keeping up to date

    Knowing about new technologies and how to integrate them into your life.
