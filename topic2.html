Ethical Computing

What is Ethics?

In a nutshell, ethics is the moral compass that directs an individual's decisions and actions throughout their lifetime. What is deemed to be right and bad for a community or society at large is included in the concept of a moral code, which transcends the boundaries of an individual.

Ethics is the study of moral decision-making, rights, obligations, language use, and what it means to live an ethical life. While moralizing may seem like an academic pursuit, it's more often an attempt to rationalize our emotions and gut feelings. Since morality is a subjective concept, many people have strong, unwavering beliefs about what is right and wrong, which can put them in stark opposition to other people's moral convictions. Morality may differ from person to person, religion to religion, and culture to culture, however many morality have been proven to be universal, originating from fundamental human feelings.

Note: Moral means conforming to the established ideas of right or wrong.

Systems of ethical conduct

  1. Relativism: There is no universal truth; moral principles are dictated by the culture and customs of society.
  2. Divine Command Theory:God is all-knowing; moral standards are perfectly stated by God’s laws.
  3. Utilitarianism:Judges actions to be right or wrong based on their consequences. Considering the greater good.
  4. Virtue ethics: Teaches that we each have an internal moral compass and should try to be a person who spontaneously follows that guide.
  5. Deontology: suggests we should all follow common moral codes and apply those to all humanity.

Law

Simply put, the law is a systematic set of universally accepted rules and regulations created by an appropriate authority such as government, which can be regional, national, or international in nature. It is used to govern the members' actions and behaviour and can be enforced by imposing penalties.

Remember that: Not all illegal behaviors are unethical.

Ethics

Ethics are the guiding principles that guide a person or society in determining what is good or bad, right or wrong in a given situation. It governs a person's behaviour or conduct and assists an individual in leading a good life by enforcing moral rules and guidelines.

Remember that: Unethical behaviors aren’t necessarily illegal.

Amoral Behavior VS Unethical Behavior

  • Amoral behavior occurs when a person has no sense of right or wrong and that person has no interest in the consequences of their action.
  • Unethical behavior is defined as the failure to conform to a set of approved standards of behavior.

Personal Ethics

What is Personal Ethics?

A person's personal ethics are the moral precepts they apply to their decision-making and behavior in both personal and professional contexts. These principles have an impact on many facets of a person's life and aid in the development of their work ethic, values, and aspirations in both their personal and professional lives. People apply their ethics to distinguish between good and wrong and to shape other people's actions under difficult circumstances. Although everyone has a different code of ethics, many people have similar values like respect and honesty.

Influence on the Personal Ethics:

  1. Family: Pertains to the cultural bias toward cetain ethical positions.Often times, people of the same blood have the same beliefs.
  2. Religion: Most religions have established codes of ethical conduct.
  3. Experiences: They say experiences are the best teacher. People pick up the things or lessons as they journey in life.
  4. Teachers: They classify as the second parent of children. During school days or season, majority of the hours of children are spent with their respective teachers.

Ethics and Technology

How is Ethics associated with Technology?

  1. Intellectual Property: It is a work that is the result of someone’s creativity and knowledge that is protected by copyright law and documented by copyrights, patents, and trademarks.
  2. Privacy: It pertains to the freedom from unauthorized intrusion.
  3. Social Justice:
    • Predictive policing: An act of gathering data from different sources, analyzing it, and then using the results to prevent future crime.
  4. Liability:
    • Liable: A person is obligated according to law or equity.
  5. Censorship:
    • Censoring: Obligated according to law or equity to examine in order to suppress or delete anything considered objectionable.
  6. Social Activism
    • Hacktivism (derived from hack and activism): Involves using computers and computer networks in a subversive way to promote an agenda usually related to causes such as free speech, human rights, or freedom of information. Essentially, it is using computer hacking to effect some sort of social change.
